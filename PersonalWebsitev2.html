About Lawrence

 I am currently a Senior at Lynbrook High, planning to major in Electrical Engineering. I am heavily involved in scientific lab research, conducting lab research at institutions such as the University of Iowa in the Rahmatalla Lab, where I tested biomechanical simulations for predicting human motion. I was also involved in the Schaffer Lab at Cornell University, writing MATLAB code to simulate blood flow changes in neural pathways during blood clots caused by neurodegenerative diseases. I have also worked on and filed patents for independent projects, such as a health monitor that can detect when respiratory blockage occurs and send out warning signals.

  Outside the realm of science, I also enjoy volunteering at the American Red Cross in my free time. Currently, I am the Health and Safety Coordinator on the Silicon Valley Red Cross Youth Executive Board, teaching Basic Aid Training classes with CPR and First-Aid skills to Cub Scout troops. I am officially certified in CPR/AED/First-Aid and I have over 200 hours of community service. I also enjoy mountain biking when I have the chance, going to spots like Fremont Older when the weather is nice.
